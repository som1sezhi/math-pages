after which Hilbert gets arrested for infinite building code violations

Infinite sets are pretty wacky compared to the stuff we're used to seeing in everyday life. One of the most famous demonstrations of the weirdness of infinite sets is Hilbert's Hotel, presumably invented by some guy named Hilbert.

The hotel

Let's say you're the manager of a hotel with a countably infinite number of rooms.

this is you

Somehow, the hotel is fully booked tonight: all $\aleph_0$ rooms are occupied, with no vacancies anywhere. So when a desperate visitor arrives in your lobby looking for a room, you're a bit stumped.

Surely, "every room is booked" means that you can't fit any more guests inside? Maybe with a puny finite hotel, but Hilbert's Hotel is special. Luckily, you've heard that Hilbert's Hotel is special, so you decide to do some online research. Eventually, you stumble across a web page describing a hotel exactly like the one you're trying to manage right now. How convenient!
